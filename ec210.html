EC210: Macroeconomic Principles

EC210 is a course in intermediate macroeconomics. It was taught by Professor Kevin Sheedy in the Michaelmas Term and Professor Ricardo Reis in the Lent Term of academic year 2016-2017. The class covers an introduction to economic growth, consumption, investment, unemployment, business cycles, inflation, monetary and fiscal policy, financial markets and international macroeconomics. The course included 20 hours of professor-led lectures and 10 hours of TA-led classes in each term. I was responsible for teaching one class.


I was awarded a Highly Commended Teaching Award for my teaching on this class.
